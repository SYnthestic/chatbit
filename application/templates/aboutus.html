What is a chatbot?

A chatbot, short for "chat robot", is a computer program that simulates human conversation, either through text or voice interactions, with users via messaging applications, websites, mobile apps or through the telephone.

Chatbots use natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) algorithms to understand user inputs and respond to them in a human-like manner.

They can perform a wide range of tasks, from providing customer support and answering frequently asked questions to ordering food, booking appointments, and even controlling smart home devices.

What is the model used?

The model used is a Convolutional Neural Network. What it does is train with multi layers, just like a real brain would on various sub-samples of your input, and uses averaging to improve the predictive accuracy and control over-fitting. Over-fitting is a bane to any Data Analyst/Scientist. As such, we strive hard to improve the accuracy of our machine learning models, without also overfitting. And Convolutional Neural Networks are a good candidate, other than Recurrent Neural Networks that is!!
